29. STAKING FACILITIES -


29.1 General


29.1.1 The terms set out in this Section 29 apply to Your use of the Staking Facilities as defined below. Please note that in the event of any conflict between this, Section 29 and the other Sections of these Terms and Conditions, the other Sections of these Terms and Conditions shall prevail.

 

29.1.2 For the purposes of this Section 29 the "Staking Facilities" means the facilities provided by Us on the partypoker staking.com online site to allow You either to buy or sell the right to receive a proportion (a "Stake Share" as defined further below) of any Winnings (as defined below) that the Player may have in a specified poker tournament or competition conducted using the Gaming Facilities in accordance with Our terms and conditions (an "Event"), such sale to be made in accordance with the terms set out in this Section.

 

29.1.3 In this Section 29 "Funder" refers to a person who has purchased a Stake Share and "Player" refers to a person who has sold a Share.

 

29.2 Relevant Terms and Conditions
Your use of the Staking Facilities shall be governed by and conducted in accordance with the generally accepted staking rules set out in this Section. Capitalised terms defined elsewhere in these Terms and Conditions bear the same meaning in this Section 29 unless the term has been defined differently in this section 29 and/or the context otherwise requires.

 

29.3 Definition of Stake Shares

 

29.3.1 A Stake Share shall constitute the sale, on the terms and subject to the conditions outlined in this Section 29 by a Player to the Funder holding such Stake Share of a right to receive an amount equal to a specified percentage (which may be expressed up to 4 decimal places) of that Player's Winnings of a specified Event.

 

29.3.2 Stake Share purchases must be made via the Staking Facilities.

 

29.4 Calculation of winnings
Winnings are defined as the amount that the Player cashes out for at the end of the Event. Winnings do not include; any physical material goods added or included in a prize pool i.e. a car or a bracelet; a trophy, any future Event seats or packages added or included into a prizepool; any promotional bonuses or rebates for taking part in specific Events; any Golden Chip or Last Longer prizes.

We reserve the right to edit or change what is to be included in the calculation of winnings at our discretion with a view to reach a fair decision to protect the interest of both Player and Funder. Such variation shall take effect in respect of Events held after the date that such variation has been reflected in a variation to this Section 29.

 

29.5 Example of the amount due to Funder in respect of his Stake Share

A Funder's share of the Winnings are calculated by multiplying the percentage Stake Share purchased by the amount of the Player's Winnings in the relevant Event. For example, if A Funder has bought a 10% Stake Share from a Player who has won $20,000 the Funder would be due 0.1*$20,000 = $2,000.

 

29.6 Pricing of Stake Shares. Stake Shares are offered, in accordance with the procedures set in section 29.10 at a price that is expressed as a percentage of the cost of the Stake payable by the Player as his or her entry price to the relevant Event. For example a Player could offer to sell a sell Stake Shares at a 20% uplift. If the cost of the Stake payable by the Player as his or her entry price to the Event is $10,000 and a Funder were to purchase a 10% Stake Share at the price offered, the agreed price for this would be £1,200 ($100,000*0.1*1.2)

 

We normally place limits on both:

  1. the maximum percentage Stake Shares that a Player may sell: We normally limit this to [80%]% to ensure that a            Player retains an incentive to play to the best of his or her skill but may impose a lower maximum for a particular            Event or for a particular Player;
  2. the maximum amount that a Player may receive in relation to the sale of Stake Shares in respect of a particular               Event: We normally limit this to 100% of the cost of the Stake payable by the Player as his or her entry price to the         Event but may impose a lower maximum for a particular Event or for a particular Player.

We will always set these rules so that a Player may not sell Stake Shares at a mark-up that would lead to the Player receiving more than the cost of the Stake payable by the Player as his or her entry price to the Event.

 

29.7 Accuracy of Purchases and Results
To ensure the highest accuracy all purchases and results, we calculate Your Account funds using four decimal points. For technical reasons, Your Account balance can only be displayed to two decimal points, which may from time to time lead to discrepancies when a value with four decimal points is rounded off to a value with two decimal points.

29.9 Sale Procedures

 

29.9.1 Offers of Stake Shares

Players who wish to use the Staking Facilities to sell Stake Shares must :

  1. ensure that they have accurately completed the minimum required information fields relative to their Account;
  2. complete truthfully the required information fields to be displayed on partypoker.co.uk for an offer to sell a Stake Share in relation to a particular Event including ticking the boxes to confirm the warranties given by the Player. If You are unable to give any of these warranties You should not offer to sell a Stake Share.

29.9.2 Editing and Cancelling of Offers

A Player has the ability to edit or cancel any offer of Stake Shares that has not already been accepted in respect of an Event at any point after submitting their offer of Stake Shares up to the last time for time before the commencement of Event at which a sale of a Stake Share may be concluded and in particular may amend:

  1. the pricing of percentage Stake Shares (which will be expressed as a percentage uplift [or discount?] to the same percentage of the cost of the Stake payable by the Player as his or her entry price to the relevant Event; and/or
  2. the minimum (if any) amount percentage of Stake Shares that must be sold in aggregate for any of the sales to become unconditional (the "Minimum Sale Condition") stipulated by the Player, however such change shall not affect the terms of any sale already concluded; and/or
  3. the total percentage Stake Shares available for sale, however such change may not be made which would cause the total percentage Stake Shares available for sale and/or sold to be less than the Minimum Sale Condition applicable in relation to any sale already concluded.

Funders who purchase a Stake Share accept any such changes in the Stake Share available occurring after the conclusion of their sale.

29.9.3 Acceptances of Offers of Stake Shares

 

29.9.3.1 Potential Funders may respond to offers to sell Stake Shares:

  1. by accepting such offer at the price advertised for of Sale Shares for any percentage (that is a whole number) up to the maximum percentage offered and not yet sold; or
  2. by bidding to purchase at a price stipulated by the Funder that is lower than the price advertised for of Stake Shares for any percentage (that is a whole number) up to the maximum percentage offered and not yet sold.

29.9.3.2 Where a potential Funder has accepted an offer in accordance with paragraph 29.11.3.1, such acceptance creates a binding contract for the sale of that Stake Share subject to the Minimum Sale Condition (if any) stipulated by the Player the time of the conclusion of such contract.

 

29.9.3.3 Where one or more Funders has bid to purchase at Stake Shares a price stipulated by the Funder that is lower than the price advertised for of Stake Shares in accordance with paragraph 29.9.3.1(b), the Player may at any time up to the last time for time before the commencement of Event at which a sale of a Stake Share may be concluded respond to offers by lowering the price of any remaining Stake Shares at which point:

  1. any Funders who have bid at a price that is equal to or above the lowered price offered by the Funder will (subject to paragraph 29.9.3.4) have such bids accepted and such acceptance creates a binding contract for the sale of that Stake Share (subject to the Minimum Sale Condition stipulated by the Player the time of the conclusion of such contract).
  2. any remaining percentage of Stake Share will then be offered at the new reduced price.

 

29.9.3.4 Where the operation of paragraph 29.9.3.3 would (except for the operation of this paragraph 29.9.3.4) cause Stake Shares to be sold that are in excess of the maximum percentage Stake Shares offered by the Player remaining for sale, then the remaining percentage Stake Shares offered shall be allocated among the bidding Funders on the following basis:

  1. first according to the price bid by such Funders, on the basis that a higher bid will be accepted in preference to a lower bid; and
  2. where bids are equal, then preference will be given to the Funder or Funders who bid earlier (on the timestamp associated with each bid).
  3. a bid or bid qualifying for acceptance under paragraph 29.9.3.3 that is not fully satisfied after the application of such allocations shall be part completed up to the remaining value of the Stake Share offered. Accordingly Funders bidding at lower than the Players stipulated price accept the possibility that should a player lower their price that their bid may be part completed for a fraction of the value of their bid.

 

29.9.4 Editing and Cancelling of Funder bids

 

29.9.4.1 A Funder may edit or cancel his or her pending bids at any point after submitting the bid, providing the time limit on the sale has not been reached and the Player has not lowered his or her stipulated price, leading to the acceptance of the bid.

 

29.9.4.2 Should a Funder edit the price on a bid to the point where it exceeds the price the Player is currently stipulating, a sale will be completed at the price that the Player has stipulated, not at the higher price bid by the Funder.

 

29.9.4.3 Should a Funder edit the price or percentage Stake Share they wish to purchase, the moment at which they edit this bid will be the new time stamp on the bid. Therefore, if there are other bids at the same price as the Funders new bid, the new bid will go to the bottom of the queue in that pool of bids.

 

29.10 Completion of a Sale

 

29.10.1 A sale of Share Stake is considered completed when the full share of the Event offered by the Player (taking account of any editing effected by the Player in accordance with paragraph 29.9.2 in the percentage Sale Share offered) is completely sold out, or where the time limit relative to the Event share expires and the Minimum Sale Condition is satisfied in relation to such sale.

 

29.10.2 When a sale is completed it is the Player's responsibility to pay the remaining balance of the Event to buy in applicable to the Event after taking account of the amounts collected from Funders before they may play the Event and the Player authorises Us to effect any payment becoming due from the Player's Account.
